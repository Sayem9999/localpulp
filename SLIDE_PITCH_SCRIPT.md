# Presentation Slide Plan & Script
**CelluPulp BD - 10 Minute Academic Pitch**

*Total Time: 10 Minutes | Team Members: 5 | Target Time per Speaker: ~2 Minutes*
*Navigate the deck using the Left/Right Arrow keys or Spacebar.*

---

## 🎤 SPEAKER 1: Md Sayem Sarkar
**Focus:** Hook the audience, define the pain point, and introduce the local solution.

### Slide 1: Hero / Title Slide
* **Visuals:** CelluPulp Logo, "6,000 TPY Pilot Facility", 3D Rotating Pulp Model.
* **Script:**
> "Good morning respected faculty. Today, our team is presenting CelluPulp BD—a strategic import substitution venture designed specifically for Bangladesh's hygiene sector."

### Slide 2: The Problem & Our Solution
* **Visuals:** Alert Triangle (Problem) vs. Shield (Solution).
* **Script:**
> "The FMCG sector for diapers and sanitary pads is booming, but there is a massive bottleneck. Every year, local manufacturers import up to 80,000 Metric Tons of wood fluff pulp. That’s $80 million dollars draining out of our economy. With the current dollar liquidity crisis, massive LC margins, and 90-day lead times, it’s crippling their cash flow.
> 
> Our solution is CelluPulp BD. We are establishing a 6,000 Tons Per Year pilot plant right here in the BEZA zone. We will produce hygiene-grade fluff pulp using highly renewable native jute and bamboo. The result? Zero forex risk, and lead times cut from 90 days to just 48 hours."

---

## 🎤 SPEAKER 2: [Team Member 2]
**Focus:** Explaining the Go-to-Market positioning and how the company actually makes money (The Margin Spread).

### Slide 3: Business Strategy
* **Visuals:** "8-10%" target market share, Tier 1 vs Tier 2 B2B positioning.
* **Script:**
> "Thank you, Sayem. We are taking a pragmatic approach to market entry. We aren't trying to monopolize the market on day one. We are targeting a highly achievable 8-10% market share. 
> 
> Our B2B strategy is twofold: For Tier 2 regional brands, we will be their Primary Supplier. For massive Tier 1 conglomerates, we offer ourselves as a strategic Secondary Supplier—giving them a risk-free way to diversify their supply chain."

### Slide 4: Revenue & Margin
* **Visuals:** "100,000 BDT/MT", "35-40% Margin", The Margin Spread Bar.
* **Script:**
> "How do we generate profit? It’s all in the margin spread. Because we source local agri-feedstock, our Cost of Goods Sold is ultra-low and strictly in Taka. Imported pulp relies on expensive USD and volatile global freight. 
> 
> Because of this massive discrepancy, we can price our product at a flat 100,000 BDT per Metric Ton—which is about 20% cheaper than imports—and still secure a healthy 35-40% gross margin at optimal capacity."

---

## 🎤 SPEAKER 3: [Team Member 3]
**Focus:** Marketing mechanics (the 4Ps) and the actual flow of manufacturing.

### Slide 5: Marketing Plan
* **Visuals:** Product image, the 4Ps, "The Trial Run Strategy".
* **Script:**
> "Moving to marketing. Our primary promotional strategy is 'The Trial Run'. Industrial buyers are risk-averse. To win them over, we subsidize initial trial runs of our pulp on their actual high-speed machines. Once they see our pulp doesn't tear and absorbs perfectly, we convert that trial into a 3-to-5 year off-take contract. We also leverage the strong ESG narrative of being 'Made with Bangladesh Fibers'."

### Slide 6: Operational Flow
* **Visuals:** Factory Image, 5-Step Flowchart from Aggregation to ZLD.
* **Script:**
> "Operationally, we run a continuous bio-digestion plant processing 16.5 MT per day. We aggregate jute and bamboo from local cooperatives, digest it, use Totally Chlorine-Free bleaching for brightness, and bale it for direct-to-manufacturer dispatch.
> 
> Crucially, we utilize a Zero-Liquid Discharge (ZLD) plant. 100% of the chemicals are recovered. We ensure zero river pollution, making us fully compliant with the Department of Environment."

## 🎤 SPEAKER 4: [Team Member 4]
**Focus:** The Financials—Investment ask, the J-Curve, and growth.

### Slide 7: The Investment
* **Visuals:** "120-150 Crore BDT" Capital Expenditure.
* **Script:**
> "To bring this to life, our initial CAPEX requirement is between 120 and 150 Crore BDT. This funding is heavily allocated towards the continuous digester machinery and the mandatory ZLD effluent plant, along with Year 1 working capital. We will finance this through a 50/50 mix of Syndicated Debt and Private Equity."

### Slide 8: 3-Year Growth Forecast
* **Visuals:** Year 1, Year 2, Year 3 Revenue projections, "4.5 Years Payback".
* **Script:**
> "Financially, we expect a classic J-Curve. In Year 1, operating at 40% capacity, we generate 240 Million BDT in revenue but expect an accounting loss due to heavy initial depreciation and interest. 
> 
> By Year 2, as trial runs convert to contracts, volume jumps by 87%, and we hit our operational Break-Even Point. By Year 3, at 90% capacity, we generate 540 Million BDT in revenue with an EBITDA of 134 Million, putting us on track for a 4.5-year project payback."

## 🎤 SPEAKER 5: [Team Member 5]
**Focus:** Team structure, Risks, and the "Plan B" Contingency.

### Slide 9: Organization Structure
* **Visuals:** Organogram showing MD, Tech Pillar, Revenue Pillar, Logistics Pillar.
* **Script:**
> "Our organizational structure is lean. The Managing Director handles strategy and capital. Below him are three critical pillars: A Plant Operations Manager overseeing the chemical engineering, a Head of B2B Sales securing contracts, and a Supply Chain Director managing our network of rural farmers."

### Slide 10: Risk & Contingency
* **Visuals:** Tech Risk, Operational Risk, "The Contingency Plan" flow.
* **Script:**
> "Finally, we must address risk. Our biggest speculative risk is technical validation—what if our early batches aren't bright or strong enough for diapers? 
> 
> We have a strict 'Plan B' Contingency Plan. If a batch fails hygiene specs, we immediately downgrade and liquidate it to the local packaging and corrugated board sector. This guarantees we recover our variable costs and prevents cash burn while our engineers recalibrate the recipe."

* **Visuals:** "THANK YOU", Rotating Pulp.
* **Script:**
> "In conclusion, CelluPulp BD isn't just a pulp mill; it's an economic shield for local FMCG manufacturers. Thank you for your time. We are now open for any questions."
