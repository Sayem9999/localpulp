## 1. Introduction

**The Pitch**
* **Problem Statement:** Bangladesh’s hygiene sector (diapers, sanitary napkins) is highly dependent on importing 60,000 to 80,000 Metric Tons of wood fluff pulp annually. With the ongoing dollar liquidity crisis and severe LC (Letter of Credit) margins, importing $80 million worth of raw materials is crippling domestic cash flows and disrupting supply chains.
* **Solution:** CelluPulp BD offers a localized solution by establishing a 6,000 Metric Ton Per Year (TPY) pilot plant to produce hygiene-grade fluff pulp from local jute and bamboo. We eliminate foreign exchange risk, cut lead times to 48 hours, and provide a product 15-25% cheaper than landed imports.

## 4. Business Model

**Business & Revenue Model Discussion**
* **Model Discussion:** We operate on a B2B "Secondary Supplier" model. We target 8-10% of the domestic market by acting as the primary supplier for Tier 2 regional brands, and a strategic, risk-free secondary supplier for Tier 1 conglomerates seeking supply chain diversification.
* **Profit Mechanics:** Revenue is generated through direct B2B sales of fluff pulp. Profit is generated through the margin spread between inexpensive local agriculture and expensive global imports. By sourcing jute/bamboo directly from local cooperatives, our Cost of Goods Sold (COGS) is strictly in Taka. Pricing our pulp 20% below imported competitors still yields a Gross Margin of ~35-40% at optimal capacity.

**a. Business Model CANVAS Summary**
* **Key Partners:** Farmer cooperatives, DoE, Tier 2 FMCG converters.
* **Key Activities:** Sustainable feedstock sourcing, bio-friendly continuous pulping.
* **Value Proposition:** 15-25% cost leadership, LC/Forex risk elimination.
* **Customer Segments:** Tier 2 regional pad producers, Tier 1 diaper manufacturers.

**b. Pricing Model**
Cost-Leadership pricing set at a flat **100,000 BDT per Metric Ton**, insulated from USD exchange rate fluctuations via BDT-denominated off-take contracts.

**c. Social Goal / Eco-friendly / Ethical Goal**
* **Financial Sustainability:** Retaining $6M-$8M USD annually within the local economy.
* **Social Goal:** Creating ~150 direct jobs and stabilizing incomes for 500+ rural farmers.
* **Eco-friendly:** Utilizing renewable non-wood fibers lowers Scope 3 carbon emissions. Operations use a mandatory Zero-Liquid Discharge (ZLD) wastewater facility.

## 8. Operational Plan

**A. Description of operation:** 24/7 continuous manufacturing (three 8-hour shifts, 330 days/year) to maximize digester efficiency.
**B. Flow of orders:** Long-term contracts dictate a predictable weekly production schedule, dispatched from a 14-day local buffer warehouse.
**C. Technology utilization:** Digital QC labs testing every batch for ISO 9001 compliance (absorbency/brightness) before release.

**Operational Flow Chart Details**
1. **Feedstock Aggregation:** Local cooperatives deliver chipped raw jute and bamboo.
2. **Continuous Digestion:** Processing 16.5 MT/day through a bio-friendly pulping process.
3. **TCF Bleaching:** Totally Chlorine-Free washing to achieve hygiene-safe brightness.
4. **Baling & Dispatch:** Pulp is dried, baled, and dispatched via D2M trucking.
5. **Zero-Liquid Discharge (ZLD):** All wastewater is filtered via membrane tech and recycled back to step 2. ZERO river pollution.

## 11. Assessment of Risk & Contingency

**A. Pure Risk:** Fire or industrial accidents. Mitigated via BEZA safety compliance and comprehensive insurance.
**B. Speculative Risk:** Technology Validation Risk (pulp tearing on high-speed machines) and ZLD operational failure leading to DoE shutdowns.
**C. Contingency Plan (Plan B - Shortly):** If initial batches fail to meet the strict parameters for *hygiene-grade* pulp, we will immediately downgrade and liquidate the pulp to the local **packaging and corrugated board sector**. This guarantees we recover our variable costs and prevents cash-burn while the engineering team recalibrates the chemical recipe.
